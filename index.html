Thinking

What I'm thinking about

A few things that have been on my mind lately — mostly at the intersection of technology, financial services, and what it actually means to build things that work.

March 2026
Why 7 million Italians are invisible to credit models
BNPL has grown 40x in Italy in six years. Scalapay, Klarna, Amazon Pay Later — millions of people are building financial histories that never touch a bank. Traditional credit scoring models were trained on data that simply doesn't capture this behaviour. The implication is uncomfortable: we're getting better at predicting risk for people who already have a history, and systematically worse at serving the ones who don't. That gap is where the next wave of fintech opportunity — and regulatory pressure — will land.
Fintech · Credit risk
February 2026
The consultant's real job in an AI world
The conversation about AI replacing consultants misses the point. What AI replaces is the part of consulting that was never really consulting — slide formatting, data aggregation, first-draft frameworks. What it doesn't replace is judgment under uncertainty: knowing when the data is telling you something real versus when the client just wants confirmation. That distinction is harder to build than it looks, and it's becoming the only thing that separates a good consultant from a very expensive search engine.
Consulting · AI
January 2026
What Puppy Yoga taught me about market validation
Before I launched, I thought the hardest part would be finding the dogs. It wasn't — it was convincing people to pay for something that didn't exist yet. We sold out the first event before we had a venue confirmed. That told me more about demand than any market analysis could have. The lesson I took into consulting: the fastest way to test an assumption isn't to model it, it's to find the smallest possible version of it and put it in front of someone who has to pay. If they do, you have something.
